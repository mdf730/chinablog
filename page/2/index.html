Visiting the Great Wall

We climbed the Great Wall of China!

We spent the full day, our day “off” from ringing a formal concert, on the Great Wall of China. It was an amazing, once in a lifetime experience (for most of us). We climbed many, many stairs up to stand on the actual wall.

And then we got to ring bells there. We packed them in backpacks surrounded by sweatshirts and socks, and all bells (and people) arrived safely when we got to the top. We played the Alma Mater atop the Great Wall of China. It was the most surreal moment of the trip.

All of our feet were aching, and we were utterly exhausted by the time we got to the bottom of the wall. But we still took the time to pick up some souvenirs. Some of us really enjoyed haggling with vendors for a good price, and helped the rest of us get a good deal on our pictures, pins, and hats. A few of us tried some street food, in the form of veggie crepes!

Oct 15, 2017 Reading time ~2 minutes

BGG Liturgy and PKU Concert

Today can be broken down into two bell performances: one at a Liturgy and one at a concert. This is pretty standard for our bell choir.

If you add another detail, today becomes so very unique and impossibly amazing.

Our liturgy was not in the Basilica of the Sacred Heart, but on the 17th floor of Genesis Beijing, home of Notre Dame’s Beijing Global Gateway! We had a view of Beijing as we rang pieces we had played in the Basilica only last month. As is pretty standard for a liturgical choir, we gathered in prayer on Sunday morning.

After our Sunday service, we headed off to Peking University before our concert there in the evening. We all looked just like the visitors on campus back in South Bend, as we took pictures of the lovely lake. We even visited the campus bookstore and acquired some PKU gear!

We had the chance to have lunch with music students from Peking University before our joint rehearsal that afternoon. We conversed about languages, hometowns, and fields of study!

The evening concert was a combination of pieces from the PKU Chinese Music Institute students and bells performances, with four pieces performed together at the end of the night. Two traditional Chinese pieces were played, and then Notre Dame, Our Mother and the PKU alma mater were performed by all!

Goodbye Notre Dame, Hello Beijing

It’s nearly 3 in the morning. CoMo 328, home of the handbell choir, is strewn with packing material, handbell cases, tablecloths, foam, and binders. The cases are labeled “Fragile” in English and Chinese. The binders are stuffed with a wide variety of music. In a few short hours, the sun will come up and the choir will gather from across campus to empty the contents of the cluttered room onto a bus. At 11 AM ET, we will be departing from campus and officially en route to Beijing, China.

The first day of the trip, October 12th, will be dedicated to getting from Notre Dame to Beijing. By 12 PM CT, we will arrive at O’Hare airport in Chicago. Our luggage includes 63 handbells, 1,290 pages of sheet music, and 10 vacuum-sealed bags of foam padding. This equipment, along with 16 travellers, will be in the air on its way to Beijing International Airport by 3:20 PM ET.

Barring unexpected delays, we will arrive in Beijing by 6:00 AM ET. All of China is 12 hours ahead of the U.S. East Coast, so it’ll be 6 PM in Beijing.

This blog will be updated as internet access permits. Please check in often to read updates and see pictures of our journey.
